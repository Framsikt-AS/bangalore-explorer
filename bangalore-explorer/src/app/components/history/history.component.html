History of Bangalore

From a small mud fort to India's tech capital

The Origins

Bangalore's documented history begins with the founding of a mud fort by Kempe Gowda I, a feudatory ruler under the Vijayanagara Empire in 1537. Kempe Gowda is often referred to as the founder of modern Bangalore.

Legend has it that Kempe Gowda was hunting in this area when a rabbit turned against his dog. This show of courage impressed him, and he decided this was a good omen and an ideal place to establish his capital.

The name "Bengaluru" has interesting origins. According to popular belief, the name comes from "Benda Kalu Uru" (Town of Boiled Beans). As the story goes, a hungry king was once served boiled beans by an old woman in this region, and he named the place "Benda Kalu Uru" in gratitude.

The Modern Transformation

The contrast between old and new Bangalore

Bangalore's transformation into India's Silicon Valley began in the 1980s when Texas Instruments became the first multinational corporation to establish a presence in the city. The economic liberalization policies of 1991 accelerated the IT boom.

The city's pleasant climate, presence of skilled workforce due to educational institutions, and proactive government policies made it an attractive destination for global tech companies.

Today, Bangalore (officially Bengaluru since 2006) is home to numerous IT companies, startups, research institutions, and a vibrant multicultural population that has transformed the once "Garden City" into a bustling metropolis.
